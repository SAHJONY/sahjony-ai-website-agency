01 — The Big Picture

What the business actually is

You sell done-for-you websites to small, local "mom-and-pop" businesses — taquerías, barbershops, salons, auto shops, florists, restaurants. Most of them either have no website, or a broken one from 2012. You give them a beautiful, mobile-ready, bilingual site in a few days, for a price a small business can say yes to.

Your unfair advantage is the AI builder: it writes every word, picks the design, and assembles a full site in seconds. What used to take an agency two weeks and $5,000, you deliver in an afternoon for under $1,300. Your real job isn't building — it's finding owners and closing them.

"Sell the result — more customers walking in the door — not the website. Nobody wants a website. Everybody wants a fuller dining room."

The money, simply

In

Customer pays $899–$1,299 to build their site, plus an optional $89–$99/month care plan.

Out

Your real cost per site is near zero — the AI builds it and Vercel hosts it free. Domains run ~$12–20/yr, billed to the customer.

Keep

Nearly the entire build fee is profit. The monthly care plan is almost pure margin.

Goal

Stack recurring monthly clients. 20 care plans ≈ $1,800/mo coming in whether you sell anything new or not.

02 — Pricing

What to charge

Three tiers. Always quote the middle one first — it's what most shops should buy, and it anchors the price.

The Corner Shop

$899

A clean one-page site: hero, services, hours, map, click-to-call. Gets them on Google.

Best for: Tiny shops, food trucks, solo operators.

The Neighborhood

$1,299

A full multi-section site: about, menu/services, gallery, reviews, FAQ, contact — bilingual.

Best for: Most businesses. Your default — quote it first.

The Cornerstone

$89/mo

Ongoing care: hosting, updates, menu & price changes, seasonal promos, priority support.

Best for: Every customer — attach it to every build.

The real money is the monthly. One build pays you once. The care plan pays you every month, forever. Push every customer toward "build + care." Ten care clients at $89/mo is ~$10,700/year on autopilot.

03 — Finding Customers

Where the leads come from

📍 The autonomous lead finder (built in)

Open the dashboard → Leads → 'Find leads near me.' It uses your location to surface nearby businesses that need a site. Work the pipeline from New → Contacted → Mockup → Closed.

🚶 Walk the neighborhood

The highest close-rate channel. Visit shops in person, phone in hand, ready to show a mockup. Local owners buy from people they meet.

🗣️ Referrals

Every happy customer knows ten other owners. After you launch a site, ask: 'Who else needs one?' Offer a free month of care for any referral that closes.

🔁 Redesign-by-URL cold outreach

Find businesses with bad/old sites, generate a better version with one click, and send it: 'Made you a new site — want it?' Showing beats telling.

The fastest start: walk your own neighborhood. Find 10 shops with no website or an ugly one. Use "Redesign by URL" on the bad ones to generate a better version before you walk in — then show the owner what their site could look like, today, on your phone.

04 — The Pitch

How to close them

Keep it human. You're a neighbor helping a neighbor look good online. Here's the flow that works:

1

Open with their world, not yours

"I love this place — do you get many folks finding you online?" Let them tell you the problem first.

2

Show, don't pitch

Pull up the mockup you already generated of THEIR business. Watching their own shop appear on screen does the selling for you.

3

Anchor on the middle tier

"Most shops like yours go with the full site at $1,299 — everything included, live this week." Then let them step down if they need to.

4

Attach the monthly

"For $89 a month I keep it updated, hosted, and add your specials — you never touch it." Frame it as peace of mind, not a fee.

5

Close with a tiny yes

"Want me to send you a free mockup by tomorrow?" Free mockup → approval → invoice. Low-friction first step closes more.

Answers to the 4 objections you'll always hear

"It's too expensive."

"It's a one-time cost less than a month of ads — and it works 24/7 bringing people in. Or we start at $899 for a simple one."

"I already have a Facebook page."

"Perfect — your site pulls the people who Google you but aren't on Facebook, and you own it. We'll link them together."

"I don't have time for this."

"That's the whole point — you do nothing. Give me your hours and a few photos, I handle everything, it's live this week."

"Let me think about it."

"Totally — let me send you a free mockup so you've got something real to think about. No cost, no commitment."

05 — Building & Delivering

From "yes" to live in a day

1

Collect the basics

Business name, type, address, phone, hours, and a few photos if they have them. That's all the AI needs.

2

Generate the site

Open the AI Builder, enter the details (or paste their old URL to redesign), pick the language, and hit build. A full site appears in seconds.

3

Add their real media

Turn on 'Add my photos/video' so the owner's real pictures, menu, or a short clip go in. Real photos close the deal.

4

Review & regenerate

Read it like the customer would. Tweak copy, regenerate sections you don't love, swap colors. Two minutes of polish.

5

Publish

Hit Publish & Share → deploy free on Vercel, or download the file. Buy them a domain right from the panel if they don't have one.

6

Hand it over & bill

Send the live link, collect the build fee, and start the monthly care plan. Log them as a client in the dashboard.

07 — Scaling Up

Turning it into a real business

🤝 Hire commission reps

Pay 25% per build + 25% of the monthly. They sell, the AI builds, you keep the recurring base. The Sales Team tab tracks everyone.

🎯 Niche down

Become 'the website person for Houston restaurants.' One industry means one pitch, one set of photos, and word spreads fast.

📦 Productize add-ons

Online ordering, booking, Google reviews setup, photography — each an easy upsell on top of the base site and monthly plan.

Your first-week checklist

1 Pick your first neighborhood or niche.
2 Generate 5 mockups for real local businesses (use Redesign-by-URL).
3 Visit or call all 5 with the mockups ready on your phone.
4 Land your first 'free mockup → yes' and publish it on Vercel.
5 Attach a monthly care plan to that first client.
6 Ask them for one referral. Repeat.
